Mothtrap · a research project by Ryan Heuser

Mothtrap is an independent client for Bluesky that draws your timeline as a map of conversations rather than a scrolling list. It's built and maintained by one person as a research project.

contact@mothtrap.blue

Write about anything — bugs, questions, privacy requests, or concerns about how the app behaves. I read everything sent here and aim to reply within a few working days.

Reporting a post or an account

Use the ⋯ menu on any post in the app. From there you can:

  • Report the post or the account, choosing a reason;
  • Mute an account, so you stop seeing them without them knowing;
  • Block an account, which is mutual and public.
Reporting from the ⋯ menu submits your report to the moderation service for your Bluesky account — the party that can actually remove a post or suspend an account. Mothtrap displays Bluesky rather than hosting it, so deciding and acting on a report is theirs; Mothtrap's job is to get it to them, and it does not receive a copy.

Mothtrap does honour the outcomes: it applies the content labels, mutes and blocks from your Bluesky account, hiding or covering posts accordingly. If you think it is failing to respect a label, mute or block, that is a bug in Mothtrap and I want to hear about it at the address above.

To report something to Bluesky directly, or to appeal a moderation decision on your own account, see Bluesky's support pages.

Content and conduct

Mothtrap has no tolerance for abusive users or for content that is illegal, harassing, or sexually exploitative. Because the content comes from Bluesky, enforcement happens through the reporting, muting and blocking above, and through the moderation services those reports reach.

Privacy

What Mothtrap stores and what leaves your device is set out on the privacy page. The short version: nearly everything stays in your browser, with one documented exception for the topic digest.
